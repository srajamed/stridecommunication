Part A: Communication Fundamentals

Why Communication Matters in Logistics

In the fast-paced world of supply chain and logistics, clear communication can mean the difference between:

  • On-time delivery vs. delays
  • Client satisfaction vs. complaints
  • Team efficiency vs. confusion
  • Career growth vs. stagnation

Remember: At Stride Logistics, we bridge gaps between traditional and modern systems. Your communication skills are the bridge between you and success.

Eye Contact: The Foundation of Trust

Why Eye Contact Matters

  • Builds trust and credibility - Essential when discussing logistics solutions
  • Shows confidence and competence - Critical for client interactions
  • Demonstrates active engagement - Important in team meetings
  • Conveys sincerity - Vital for building professional relationships

✅ DO

  • Maintain 60-70% eye contact during conversations
  • Look at the interviewer when answering questions
  • Make brief eye contact with all participants in group meetings
  • Use the "triangle technique" - eyes, nose, mouth

❌ DON'T

  • Stare continuously (makes people uncomfortable)
  • Look at your phone or computer while speaking
  • Avoid eye contact completely (appears disinterested)
  • Look away when discussing important points

Body Language: Your Silent Communicator

The 55-38-7 Rule

55% Body language and visual cues

38% Tone of voice

7% Actual words spoken

Professional Posture Guidelines

  • Stand tall - Shoulders back, chin up
  • Sit forward - Shows engagement in meetings
  • Open gestures - Avoid crossed arms
  • Firm handshake - 2-3 seconds, web-to-web contact
  • Appropriate space - Maintain 3-4 feet in professional settings

Logistics Tip: When presenting shipping solutions or discussing freight challenges, confident body language reinforces your expertise.

Active Listening: The Key to Understanding

Why Active Listening is Critical in Logistics

  • Prevents costly misunderstandings in shipping instructions
  • Builds stronger client relationships
  • Helps identify hidden problems in supply chain processes
  • Shows respect for colleagues and supervisors

The SOLER Technique

S - Square

Face the speaker directly

O - Open

Keep an open posture

L - Lean

Lean in slightly to show interest

E - Eye

Maintain appropriate eye contact

R - Relax

Stay calm and focused

Small Talk: Building Professional Bridges

Why Small Talk Matters

Small talk is the foundation of professional relationships. It helps you:

  • Build rapport with clients before discussing logistics solutions
  • Create comfortable environments for negotiations
  • Establish trust in international business relationships
  • Network effectively at industry events

✅ SAFE TOPICS

  • Weather and current events
  • Professional interests and industry trends
  • Travel (especially relevant in logistics)
  • Local restaurants and culture
  • Recent achievements or projects

❌ AVOID

  • Personal finances
  • Political opinions
  • Personal health issues
  • Negative gossip about colleagues
  • Controversial social topics

English Fluency: Your Global Advantage

Why English Mastery is Essential

Stride Logistics operates globally - English is the universal business language in logistics, connecting you with clients across continents.

Daily Practice Strategies

  • Think in English - Practice internal dialogue in English
  • Read logistics industry publications - Supply Chain Management Review, Logistics Management
  • Listen to business podcasts - Focus on logistics and supply chain content
  • Practice with colleagues - Use English in internal communications
  • Record yourself - Practice presentations and listen back

Goal: Speak with confidence about freight forwarding, cargo tracking, and supply chain optimization in clear, professional English.

Interview Excellence: Structure is Key

The STAR Method for Behavioral Questions

S - Situation

Set the context and background

T - Task

Describe the challenge or responsibility

A - Action

Explain what you did

R - Result

Share the positive outcome

Example for Logistics Role:

Situation: "Our client's shipment was delayed at port due to documentation issues..."

Task: "I needed to resolve this quickly to prevent further delays..."

Action: "I contacted the customs broker, coordinated with the shipping line..."

Result: "The shipment cleared within 24 hours, saving the client $50,000 in penalties."

Professional Communication Protocols

Email Excellence

✅ EMAIL BEST PRACTICES

  • Clear, specific subject lines
  • Professional greeting and closing
  • Concise, structured content
  • Proofread before sending
  • Respond within 24 hours

❌ EMAIL MISTAKES

  • All caps (appears aggressive)
  • Too casual language
  • Reply all unnecessarily
  • Unclear requests or questions
  • Grammar and spelling errors

Essential Corporate Phrases

  • "I wanted to follow up on..."
  • "Please let me know if you need any clarification..."
  • "I look forward to your response..."
  • "Thank you for bringing this to my attention..."

Confident Communication: Structure Your Success

The PEP Formula for Clear Communication

P - Point

State your main message clearly

E - Evidence

Provide supporting details

P - Point

Restate your conclusion

Building Structured Sentences

  • Use transition words: "Furthermore," "However," "As a result"
  • Be specific: "reduced costs by 15%" instead of "saved money"
  • Use active voice: "I managed the project" not "The project was managed"
  • Pause for emphasis: Allow important points to sink in

Logistics Example: "Our new tracking system has improved delivery accuracy. Evidence shows we've reduced lost shipments by 25% and increased customer satisfaction scores by 30%. This tracking system is clearly delivering measurable value."

The Perfect Professional Introduction

Handshake Mastery

The 3-Second Rule

  • Web-to-web contact - Palm to palm, not fingertips
  • 2-3 firm pumps - Not too many, not too few
  • Maintain eye contact - Shows confidence and respect
  • Stand up if seated - Shows respect and professionalism

Introduction Structure

1. Greeting: "Good morning/afternoon"

2. Name: "I'm [First Name Last Name]"

3. Role: "I'm a [Position] at Stride Logistics"

4. Value: "I specialize in optimizing freight operations"

5. Engagement: "How can I help you today?"

Networking: Building Your Professional Network

Why Networking Matters in Logistics

  • Logistics is relationship-driven - carriers, shippers, brokers
  • Information sharing prevents supply chain disruptions
  • Referrals drive 85% of logistics business
  • Industry knowledge spreads through professional networks

Networking Strategy

✅ EFFECTIVE NETWORKING

  • Ask about their logistics challenges
  • Share relevant industry insights
  • Exchange business cards professionally
  • Follow up within 48 hours
  • Offer help before asking for favors

❌ NETWORKING MISTAKES

  • Talking only about yourself
  • Interrupting conversations
  • Being pushy or aggressive
  • Forgetting names immediately
  • Not following up

Articulating Your Value Proposition

How to Talk About Your Achievements

The CAR Method

C - Challenge: What problem did you face?

A - Action: What did you do about it?

R - Result: What was the positive outcome?

Quantify Your Impact

  • Instead of: "I improved delivery times"
  • Say: "I reduced average delivery times by 3 days, improving customer satisfaction by 40%"

Logistics-Specific Achievement Examples

  • "Optimized routing algorithms, reducing fuel costs by 20%"
  • "Implemented new inventory system, decreasing stockouts by 35%"
  • "Negotiated with carriers, saving $500K annually in shipping costs"
  • "Streamlined customs processes, reducing clearance time by 50%"

Corporate Culture: Getting Things Done

Direct but Respectful Communication

In corporate logistics, time is money. Be clear, concise, and actionable while maintaining professionalism.

Effective Corporate Phrases

  • "Let's align on next steps..."
  • "I'll circle back with an update by..."
  • "Can we deep dive into this issue?"
  • "Let's touch base offline to discuss..."
  • "I'll take point on this initiative..."

Being Upfront and Solution-Oriented

  • Identify problems early: "I've identified a potential delay..."
  • Present solutions: "Here are three options to resolve this..."
  • Take ownership: "I'll ensure this gets resolved by..."
  • Communicate progress: "Here's where we stand on the project..."

Professional Communication: Complete Guide

✅ ALWAYS DO

  • Arrive 5 minutes early
  • Dress one level above the dress code
  • Use professional email signatures
  • Send thank you notes after meetings
  • Ask clarifying questions
  • Keep confidential information secure
  • Speak clearly and at appropriate volume
  • Use people's names in conversation

❌ NEVER DO

  • Interrupt others while speaking
  • Use slang in professional settings
  • Check phone during conversations
  • Gossip about colleagues or clients
  • Make promises you can't keep
  • Ignore cultural differences
  • Speak negatively about competitors
  • Forget to follow up on commitments

Homework Assignment

Interview Response Preparation

Due: Next session - We will review and provide feedback

Format: Written responses using STAR method

Prepare STAR Method Responses for These Questions:

  • Question 1: "Tell me about yourself"
  • Question 2: "Why are you interested in this logistics position?"
  • Question 3: "Describe a time when you solved a complex problem"
  • Question 4: "How do you handle working under pressure?"
  • Question 5: "Describe how you would explain logistics concepts to a client"

Instructions: Write 150-200 words for each response using the STAR format. Focus on logistics-related examples where possible.

Interview Response Templates

Template 1: "Tell Me About Yourself"

Structure: Present + Past + Future

Present: "I'm currently a [role] at [company] where I [key responsibility]"

Past: "I have [X years] experience in [relevant area] where I [major achievement]"

Future: "I'm looking to [career goal] and believe this role at [company] is perfect because [reason]"

Template 2: Client Interaction Questions

Framework: Listen + Understand + Explain + Confirm

  • "First, I listen carefully to understand their specific needs"
  • "I ask clarifying questions to ensure I fully understand"
  • "I explain the solution in simple, clear terms"
  • "I confirm they understand and address any concerns"

Common Logistics Interview Questions

Part A: Communication Fundamentals

Why Communication Matters in Logistics

In the fast-paced world of supply chain and logistics, clear communication can mean the difference between:

  • On-time delivery vs. delays
  • Client satisfaction vs. complaints
  • Team efficiency vs. confusion
  • Career growth vs. stagnation

Remember: At Stride Logistics, we bridge gaps between traditional and modern systems. Your communication skills are the bridge between you and success.

Eye Contact: The Foundation of Trust

Why Eye Contact Matters

  • Builds trust and credibility - Essential when discussing logistics solutions
  • Shows confidence and competence - Critical for client interactions
  • Demonstrates active engagement - Important in team meetings
  • Conveys sincerity - Vital for building professional relationships

‚úÖ DO

  • Maintain 60-70% eye contact during conversations
  • Look at the interviewer when answering questions
  • Make brief eye contact with all participants in group meetings
  • Use the "triangle technique" - eyes, nose, mouth

‚ùå DON'T

  • Stare continuously (makes people uncomfortable)
  • Look at your phone or computer while speaking
  • Avoid eye contact completely (appears disinterested)
  • Look away when discussing important points

Body Language: Your Silent Communicator

The 55-38-7 Rule

55% Body language and visual cues

38% Tone of voice

7% Actual words spoken

Professional Posture Guidelines

  • Stand tall - Shoulders back, chin up
  • Sit forward - Shows engagement in meetings
  • Open gestures - Avoid crossed arms
  • Firm handshake - 2-3 seconds, web-to-web contact
  • Appropriate space - Maintain 3-4 feet in professional settings

Logistics Tip: When presenting shipping solutions or discussing freight challenges, confident body language reinforces your expertise.

Active Listening: The Key to Understanding

Why Active Listening is Critical in Logistics

  • Prevents costly misunderstandings in shipping instructions
  • Builds stronger client relationships
  • Helps identify hidden problems in supply chain processes
  • Shows respect for colleagues and supervisors

Set the context and background

Describe the challenge or responsibility

A - Action

Explain what you did

Share the positive outcome

Making Complex Topics Simple

The KISS Principle: Keep It Simple, Smart

The Pyramid Principle

Start with conclusion: Lead with your main message

Support with evidence: Provide 2-3 supporting points

Add details: Include specifics only if needed

Logistics Example: Explaining Route Optimization

‚ùå Complex version: "Our algorithmic approach utilizes multi-variable optimization matrices considering traffic patterns, fuel consumption coefficients, and delivery time windows..."

‚úÖ Simple version: "Our new system finds the fastest, cheapest routes by analyzing traffic, fuel costs, and delivery deadlines. Result: 20% faster deliveries and 15% cost savings."

Simplification Techniques

  • Use analogies: "Think of our logistics network like a highway system..."
  • Visual metaphors: "Supply chain bottlenecks are like traffic jams..."
  • Concrete examples: "For a shipment from Mumbai to Chennai..."
  • Numbers that matter: Focus on business impact, not technical specs
